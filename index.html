WHY RUMPLEWOOD?
17 August 2025
I've never made a website and I thought it would be a fun learning experience Step 01: last week I visited lola.com just to see what was there Step 02: lola.com turned out to be a crappy dating website that by a crazy coincidence had folded their business that week and were putting their domain name lola.com FOR SALE THE VERY NEXT DAY. This was obviously a sign from god that my destiny was to own lola.com Step 03: I wrote lola.com a short email asking what the price is. I opened negotiations by offering $350 Step 04: while waiting for them to respond, I started looking into other website names as a backup Step 05: I saw that lolaworld.com was also for sale, and I eagerly clicked through to the price Step 06: I saw that the price was $8749.99 Step 07: I continued to research, and quickly realized that the price for lola.com was going to be well over one hundred thousand dollars. This was far more than I was willing to pay Step 08: I remembered, again, that we live in a fallen world---an obscene world Step 09: I made a new plan: I bought a domain name! I bought LOLA DOME dot com for nine dollars. I just liked how it sounded. It was like, welcome to the Lola Dome! Step 10: I then spent an amount of time learning how to make a website Step 11: I made the website and then realized---very suddenly---that loladome.com wouldn't work as a name. This happened when my eyes and brain finally worked together and figured out that loladome.com smooshed together into one word looks like "LOLA, DO ME!!" and that I now owned LOLA DO ME dot com Step 12: I quickly invested in a new domain name: I bought lola17.com, for nine dollars Step 13: then last night I was out for a beer, and I told a friend the happy news. I told her that I had built a new website, and it was called lola17.com. She replied, "lola17? that's awesome! is it a porn site? is 17 even legal?" Step 14: I finally hear back from lola.com. They have rejected my offer of $350 Step 15: I scrap all ideas of having a domain name with the word LOLA in it Step 16: I close my eyes and think back to a simpler time, ten years ago, when a buddy of mine asked me to name his big house that is in the hills and trees of California. I guess he wanted it to have a name like big houses do in Jane Austen books. I thought about it for a whole day and then suggested a name. His family was thrilled and said thank you for the suggestion, but we are going to go in a different direction Step 17: their loss is our gain: welcome to rumplewood
